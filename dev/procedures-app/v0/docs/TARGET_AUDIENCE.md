# Target Audience & Use Cases

## Primary User Segments

### 1. Productivity-Focused Individuals

**Demographics**
- Age: 25-45
- Tech-savvy but not necessarily developers
- Value efficiency and optimization
- Active on productivity communities (Reddit, Hacker News, productivity blogs)

**Pain Points**
- Feel overwhelmed by recurring household tasks
- Forget steps in complex routines
- Want to optimize time but lack data to know where to improve
- Struggle to maintain consistency in personal habits

**Use Cases**
- Weekly house cleaning routines
- Meal prep procedures
- Workout warmup/cooldown sequences
- Packing lists for travel
- Morning/evening routines
- Monthly financial reviews
- Car maintenance checklists

**Value Proposition**
Reduce mental load and ensure you never miss a step in your personal routines. Track your time to identify where you can optimize.

---

### 2. Small Business Owners

**Demographics**
- Small businesses with 2-20 employees
- Service-based businesses (cleaning, restaurants, clinics, retail)
- Limited IT infrastructure but need process consistency
- Often owner-operators wearing multiple hats

**Pain Points**
- Training new employees is time-consuming and inconsistent
- Quality varies depending on who's working
- No easy way to track if procedures are being followed
- Institutional knowledge lives only in people's heads
- Scaling is difficult without standardized processes

**Use Cases**
- Opening/closing procedures for retail stores
- Kitchen prep and cleaning in restaurants
- Onboarding new team members
- Client intake procedures
- Quality control checklists
- Equipment maintenance routines
- Safety protocols and compliance checks

**Value Proposition**
Standardize your operations, train new staff faster, and ensure consistent quality regardless of who's on shift.

---

### 3. Remote Teams & Distributed Organizations

**Demographics**
- Fully remote or hybrid teams (5-50 people)
- Tech startups, agencies, consulting firms
- Need structured processes without heavyweight tools
- Value transparency and shared knowledge

**Pain Points**
- Hard to ensure consistent execution across distributed team
- Onboarding remote employees takes longer
- Knowledge silos when team members leave
- Difficult to measure process efficiency
- Existing tools (Notion, Confluence) are too freeform

**Use Cases**
- Software deployment procedures
- Customer support escalation workflows
- Monthly reporting procedures
- Incident response runbooks
- Code review checklists
- Client onboarding workflows
- Security audit procedures

**Value Proposition**
Document tribal knowledge, ensure every team member follows the same process, and gather data to improve efficiency.

## Market Sizing

### Total Addressable Market (TAM)
- **Productivity app users globally**: ~500M people use productivity tools
- **Small businesses globally**: ~300M small businesses worldwide
- **Remote workers**: ~150M full-time remote workers

### Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)
- **English-speaking productivity enthusiasts**: ~50M active users
- **Small service businesses (US/EU)**: ~30M businesses
- **Remote teams with process needs**: ~10M teams

### Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM) - Year 1
- **Target**: 10,000 active users
  - 5,000 individual productivity users
  - 3,000 small business users
  - 2,000 team/enterprise users

## Go-To-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Individual Users
- Launch on Product Hunt, Hacker News, Reddit (r/productivity, r/getdisciplined)
- Content marketing: blog posts on routine optimization
- Free tier with unlimited procedures
- Word of mouth and organic growth

### Phase 2: Small Businesses
- Case studies from early business adopters
- LinkedIn outreach to small business owners
- Partnerships with small business associations
- Freemium model with team features in paid tier

### Phase 3: Enterprise/Teams
- Enhanced features for compliance and audit trails
- Sales team for enterprise deals
- Integration with existing tools (Slack, Teams, Jira)
- Custom deployment options
